The Challenge

Where automotive enterprises lose time, revenue, and customer trust.

The biggest technology problems in automotive aren't in the vehicle — they're in the enterprise systems, dealer networks, and customer touchpoints that surround it.

01
After-Sales is Reactive and Manual
Warranty claims take weeks. Technicians rely on tribal knowledge for diagnostics — cross-referencing paper manuals, calling experts, and guessing. Customer queries about their vehicles go to call centres that look things up in the same fragmented systems. High cost, low satisfaction, slow resolution.
02
Dealer Network Visibility is Fragmented
OEMs often lack real-time visibility into dealer inventory, service pipeline, and customer engagement across their network. Data sits in dealer management systems that don't talk to each other or to the OEM's own platforms — making demand planning and service quality management reactive and slow.
03
Product Knowledge is Locked in Experts
Service manuals, repair guidelines, fault histories, and technical service bulletins are spread across PLM, DMS, and SharePoint. New technicians take months to become useful. When experienced engineers leave, their knowledge leaves too. The cost of that expertise gap shows up directly in first-call resolution rates.

Industry Trends

Where automotive enterprise technology is heading.

The OEMs investing in AI and cloud now will have a structural advantage in service quality, cost efficiency, and customer loyalty within 18 months.

Trend 01
Multimodal AI in Service & Field Operations
Text-only AI is giving way to multimodal systems in automotive service — where a technician photographs a component and the AI cross-references service manuals, warranty records, and fault histories to diagnose instantly. OEMs deploying this are seeing dramatic improvements in first-call resolution and technician onboarding speed.
Trend 02
AI-Native Dealer & Customer Engagement
Voice-based service booking, proactive maintenance reminders, and 24/7 conversational AI for vehicle queries are becoming table stakes in customer-facing automotive experiences. OEMs that deploy these at scale reduce call centre costs while improving CSAT — simultaneously.
Trend 03
AI in SDLC — Custom Apps in Days, Not Months
Business users at automotive OEMs are beginning to describe workflows in natural language and receive working application MVPs — tested and validated — before handing to engineering for production. This is collapsing the time from business idea to deployed tool from months to days.
