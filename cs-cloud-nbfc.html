The Challenge

Legacy infrastructure holding back a growing business

For an NBFC operating at scale, the infrastructure is the business. Loan origination, customer onboarding, repayment processing — all of it depends on systems that need to be fast, reliable, and available around the clock. This firm's infrastructure was none of those things consistently.

On-premise hardware was approaching end-of-life. Engineering teams were operating in silos. Production incidents were invisible until customers reported them. The gap between where the platform was and where the business needed it to be was widening with every passing quarter.

Challenge 01
Ageing Infrastructure, Rising Costs
On-premise infrastructure was approaching end-of-life. Maintenance costs were increasing, hardware refresh cycles were unpredictable, and scaling capacity required weeks of procurement lead time.
Challenge 02
Siloed Teams, Slow Releases
No shared delivery platform meant each team operated its own release process. Release cycles were measured in weeks. Deployments required manual co-ordination, increasing the risk of human error.
Challenge 03
No Production Observability
Production incidents were typically reported by customers before engineering teams were aware. There was no metrics stack, no distributed tracing, and no structured log aggregation — making diagnosis slow and painful.
The Approach

Phased migration. Zero risk. No big-bang cutovers.

01
Migration Strategy & Risk Planning
Assessed all applications for cloud readiness, defined migration priority based on risk and business criticality, and built a phased migration plan with full rollback capability for every workload. Every team knew what was moving, when, and what the fallback was.
02
Zero-Downtime Migration Execution
Migrated business-critical applications to cloud using blue-green deployment strategies, database replication with live sync, and traffic cutover with instant rollback capability. Not a single customer-facing service experienced unplanned downtime through the migration window.
03
Kubernetes Delivery Platform
Built a Kubernetes-native delivery platform: shared CI/CD pipelines, Helm-managed workloads, GitOps-driven deployments, and environment promotion gates from dev to production. Every team on a single platform — one way to ship, regardless of service or stack.
04
Full Observability Stack
Deployed end-to-end observability: Prometheus and Grafana for metrics and SLO dashboards, OpenTelemetry for distributed tracing, and centralised structured log aggregation with automated alerting. Production is now visible before customers ever notice a problem.

The engineering organisation went from planning releases days in advance to shipping multiple times per day. Incidents that previously went undetected until customers called are now surfaced within minutes by automated SLO alerts. The infrastructure cost reduction funded the next phase of product investment.

The migration was not just a lift-and-shift. It was a re-platforming of how the entire engineering organisation operates. The Kubernetes delivery platform standardised how every team ships code. The observability stack gave engineering the visibility it never had on-premise. The cost savings created headroom for the next product build cycle — without waiting for a budget cycle to catch up.
