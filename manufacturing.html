The Challenge

Where manufacturing enterprises lose time, money, and knowledge

The biggest operational challenges in manufacturing aren't on the shop floor — they're in the systems, processes, and siloed knowledge that surrounds it.

Knowledge Trapped in Systems

Design guidelines live in PLM, SOPs in SharePoint, vendor contracts in ERP, service histories in DMS — all disconnected. Every cross-system answer requires a specialist and costs a day. Iterations take weeks; prototyping costs stay high; new engineers take months to become useful.

Supply Chain Running on Guesswork

Demand forecasting errors create excess stock or stock-outs. Tender and RFP cycles are manual. Supplier compliance checks are periodic reviews, not continuous signals. BOM management is slow, fragmented, and error-prone — especially across multi-tier global suppliers.

After-Sales Support is Reactive and Expensive

Warranty claims take weeks to process. Field technicians rely on tribal knowledge for diagnostics. Customer queries about equipment — especially in tractor and auto — go to call centres that look things up in the same fragmented systems. High training costs compound the problem as expertise walks out the door.

Industry Trends

Where manufacturing enterprise technology is heading

The companies investing in enterprise AI and cloud now will have a structural advantage within 18 months. These are the shifts defining the roadmap.

Trend 01
GenAI Agents Replacing Enterprise Workflows

Manufacturing enterprises are deploying GenAI agents that navigate between PLM, ERP, and SCM in a single query — handling tasks that previously required multiple specialists and days of effort. Tender agents, vendor evaluation AI, and compliance assistants are moving from pilot to production.

Trend 02
AI in SDLC — Business Users Shipping MVPs

A new pattern is emerging: business users describe a manufacturing workflow in natural language, and an AI-assisted development process generates a working MVP — tested and validated — before being handed to engineering for production hardening. This is collapsing custom app development timelines from months to days.

Trend 03
Multimodal AI in Service & Aftermarket

Text-only AI is giving way to multimodal systems in service and repair — where a field technician photographs a component and the AI cross-references service manuals, warranty records, and fault histories to diagnose the issue instantly. For tractor and auto OEMs, this is transforming first-call resolution rates.
